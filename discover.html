Our Mission & Vision

Educating, conserving, and uncovering the truth about the wildlife created to share our world.

What Makes a Besharse Field Guide?

At Besharse Field Guides, we provide the most comprehensive, expert-level information about wildlife. We focus on clear, accurate education and identification. Our field guides are meticulously structured to provide a total understanding of the species, ensuring you have the complete, scientifically sound picture in your hands.

We focus on precision range and habitat mapping, utilizing up-to-date regional data to show you exactly where a species lives and the specific micro-habitats they prefer. This ensures you can identify not just what an animal is, but where it belongs in our local landscapes. We also dig deep into the historical and cultural context of our subjects, exploring the origins of myths, fear-based folklore, and historical misconceptions that have plagued species like snakes and spiders for centuries.

Our guides provide detailed anatomical and identification keys through high-resolution breakdowns of physical characteristics, covering everything from scale counts and pattern variations in snakes to ocular arrangements and silk-spinning behaviors in spiders. We emphasize the ecological significance and benefit of each creature, offering a deep dive into the vital roles they play in our environment by naturally managing pest populations and contributing to the health of the ecosystems they inhabit. Finally, we provide guidance on conservation status and ethical interaction, showing you how to safely observe these animals in the wild, the current conservation threats they face, and how you can actively participate in their protection.

My journey began when I was just 10 years old. Over the last decade, I have dedicated my life to the study, documentation, and conservation of wildlife, with a primary focus on the species often misunderstood by society. I believe that every creature has a purpose and a place in creation, designed intentionally by God. My goal is to use this work to dispel the myths, irrational fears, and misinformation that have persisted for generations.

Addressing Myths and Conservation

Too often, fear of snakes and spiders stems from a lack of understanding of their history and their true nature. My work delves into the origins of these fears, uncovering the misconceptions that have prevented people from appreciating these animals. Many of these species are vital to our environment, helping to control pests and maintain balance in the ecosystems they inhabit. Through these field guides, I strive to show that these animals are not here to harm or cause fear, but to fulfill their unique roles in the natural order. Whether I am researching the historical significance of a species or documenting a specific specimen in the wild, my experience continues to grow as I refine our guides. Thank you for joining me on this mission to conserve wildlife and educate the public on the beauty of God’s creation.
