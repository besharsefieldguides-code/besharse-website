Our Origins & Philosophy

Rooted in Monette, Arkansas. Built for the natural world.

Founded in Monette, Arkansas

Besharse Field Guides was born out of a lifelong dedication to the natural world. Situated in Monette, Arkansas, our roots are deeply tied to the rich, diverse ecosystems of the region. Growing up surrounded by wildlife taught us that understanding nature requires more than casual observation—it demands immersion, respect, and a commitment to truth over folklore.

From the beginning, our organization has operated on a simple premise: education is the strongest tool we have for conservation. The more accurately we can document and describe the creatures that share our environment, the less fear and misunderstanding will dictate human interactions with them. What started as personal field study has grown into an expansive mission to document, catalog, and protect wildlife across the globe.

The Besharse Standard

A field guide should be an uncompromising source of truth. At Besharse Field Guides, our research methodology is built on exhaustive field work, anatomical precision, and an unwavering commitment to scientific integrity. We spend countless hours in varied habitats tracking, observing, and recording the behaviors and characteristics of the species we cover. Our data isn't just aggregated from existing literature; it is verified in the field.

We hold ourselves to an expert-level standard because our readers—from seasoned biologists to everyday nature enthusiasts—rely on us for exactness. Whether you are identifying a specific scale pattern on a snake or tracing the migratory routes of local bird populations, you can trust that the information provided has been rigorously vetted.

A Commitment to the Community

Our work extends beyond the pages of our guides. We are actively engaged in community outreach, local education, and conservation initiatives. We believe that by teaching people how to safely and respectfully interact with wildlife, we can foster a generation of stewards who will protect these habitats for years to come.

We regularly partner with local organizations, host educational events, and provide resources to schools to ensure that accurate wildlife information is accessible to everyone. Our ultimate vision involves creating a larger infrastructure for wildlife rescue, rehabilitation, and public education, expanding our reach far beyond our Arkansas origins.
